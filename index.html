WHAT LIES BENEATH

  1. How can identity be expressed? (ie traditions, languages, spirituality, beliefs, etc) How can parts of our identity be visible and other parts hidden?

    Our identity is and isn't visible from the outside world. Our identity is a mixture of reference points we use to image ourselves. I identify myself as Pakistani, Muslim, etc. People can see me as brown, male, etc. I think people tend to use these reference points to draw a picture about themselves that they would call their "identity". But it's just a drawing; their identity is something completely different. Our real identity is something far more complex and changing forever. As I stated before, our identity is and isn’t visible. People can see me as brown and male. But they can't see who I am on the inside; they can't see my love for cats, my intelligence, my kindness, and my culture and traditions. Our invisible identity refers to the characteristics that cannot be readily seen such as disability, religion, and cultural background. Our invisible identity encapsulates our backgrounds, experiences, and characteristics that make us truly unique individuals. When we experience different places, our identities change—often in response to the variety of people we meet. Whether at home or away, globalization causes us to meet people whose identities may be rooted in distant places. Our engagement with people from afar will influence how we build and reshape our own identities.

  2. How can media impact identity?
    • How does media and/or globalization perpetuate stereotypes based on your identity/characteristics?

      Media can affect our identity in many ways; one of the ways is by creating stereotypes. People now think that things like interests, communities, and beliefs are irrelevant or pointless just because they don't see these things in a feed curated specifically for them. Something weird is that we've never been more connected than this and alone at the same time. All conversation topics are about what people saw on their feeds that day, and that just makes it harder to get to know someone better. People instill their identities with the echo chamber of content they're fed every day. It's nothing new, but it is extremely alarming. Compared to people who watch too many movies/TV shows or read too many books. But at least there are more interesting things to talk about there.

    • How can media and/or globalization help promote the identity "beneath?"

      The media has a powerful say on how people view and treat different groups based on gender and ethnicity, as news stories shape people's stereotypes, beliefs, and ultimately behaviors in areas such as education, family, and politics. It is, therefore, essential to understand how the media, and in particular news outlets, depict different groups and whether stereotypes drive these depictions. The media can have an impact on self-image; this is because individuals from underrepresented groups. Media representation can influence their self-esteem and self-identity. Seeing characters who share their cultural background succeed and thrive can provide a sense of validation and belonging. In 2016, researchers found that prolonged television exposure shows a decrease in self-esteem for all girls and for black boys, but an increase in self-esteem for white boys. These differences correlate with the racial and gender practices in Hollywood, which predominantly casts white men as heroes, while erasing or subordinating other groups as villains, sidekicks, and sexual objects. The media can also create a perception of otherness that portrays certain cultures or ethnicities as exotic or "other" can contribute to a sense of separation and difference, perpetuating an "us vs. them" mentality.

  3. How can a person maintain their sense of individual identity in a globalized world?

    In a globalized and increasingly interconnected world, it can be challenging for individuals to maintain their unique identities. However, there are several strategies that individuals can use to preserve and celebrate their unique identity. People can maintain their individual identity by focusing on cultural traditions and values, seeking out community, and exploring and celebrating differences. Keeping in touch with cultural traditions and values can help individuals feel connected to their heritage and can help maintain one’s sense of individuality. They could also join groups or communities that share similar cultural traditions or values and can provide support and a sense of belonging. Embracing and celebrating differences, whether cultural, ethnic, or individual, can help individuals maintain their unique identities and foster a sense of individuality.

    Additionally, we can maintain our individual identities by not letting those we surround ourselves with have a major influence on who we are. It is omnipresent that the people we surround ourselves with will influence who we are; however, we need to have confidence within ourselves to stay authentic to our true selves. We can also see this by the resistance of the Lubicon Cree after the Alberta Government stopped taking in their opinion and took their indigenous rights away; the Lubicon Cree didn't give in to social pressures and remained true to their identity, standing up for themselves.
